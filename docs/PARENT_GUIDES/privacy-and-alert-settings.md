# Privacy & alert settings (give a teen room without going blind)

Monitoring a 9-year-old and a 16-year-old shouldn't look the same. GuardianNode
lets you dial in, per child, **what's worth an alert, what's just recorded
quietly, and what's ignored entirely** — plus how aggressively their screen is
captured. Older kids get real privacy; the serious stuff still reaches you.

Open the dashboard → **Profiles** → expand a child → **Privacy & alert settings**.

## The three behaviors

For each kind of content you choose one of:

- **Alert me** — it shows up in the Risk Feed and (for high/critical) emails you.
- **Monitor quietly** — it's recorded in the feed, but you're **not** notified.
  Use this when you want a record but not a ping (e.g. mild bullying for a teen).
- **Allow (ignore)** — not flagged at all (e.g. swearing for a 16-year-old).

You can also set **"only when"** a minimum severity per item — for example set
*Sexual or romantic talk* to **alert at Critical only**, so a private chat with a
boyfriend or girlfriend stays private unless it turns into something serious.

## Always-on safety (you can't turn these off)

Some things always alert, at every age, no matter how you set the rest:

- **Self-harm** signals
- **Grooming** (an adult/stranger working on your child)
- **Threats**
- **Sexual exploitation of a child**
- **Your own watch phrases** (the name/address/school you added)

This is deliberate — privacy settings should never be able to silence the
things that keep a kid safe.

## Age defaults (a sensible starting point)

When you create a profile, GuardianNode seeds it based on age. You can change any
of it, or hit **Reset to age defaults** at any time.

| | Under 10 | 10–13 | 14–17 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Swearing | Alert | Monitor | **Allowed** |
| Bullying | Alert | Alert | Monitor (alert if serious) |
| Romantic/sexual talk | Alert | Alert at High | **Alert at Critical only** (private unless serious) |
| Drug/alcohol mentions | Alert | Alert | Monitor |
| Alert me at or above | Low | Medium | High |

The idea: a 16-year-old swearing or trading trash talk isn't an emergency; a
private relationship stays private — but grooming, self-harm, scams, and
identity exposure still come straight to you.

## How much to capture

The same panel has **How much to capture**:

- **Tight** — frequent capture, catches small changes. Most coverage, least
  privacy. Good for younger kids.
- **Balanced** — the default.
- **Leeway** — captures less often, only meaningful screen changes. More privacy
  for an older, trusted teen.

You change this from the dashboard; the child's PC picks it up automatically
within a couple of minutes — nothing to install or reconfigure on their machine.

## Tip

Start from the age default, then loosen the one or two things you trust your kid
with. The locked safety categories mean you can be generous elsewhere without
worrying you've created a blind spot for the things that matter.
